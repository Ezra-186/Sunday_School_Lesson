What is D&C 88?
Brief History

D&C 88 is a revelation received by Joseph Smith in Kirtland, Ohio.
The main portion (verses 1–126) came on December 27–28, 1832,
with a second portion (verses 127–137) on January 3, 1833.
Joseph later called it the “olive leaf … [the Lord’s] message of peace to us.”

  • Calls the Saints to seek learning—“by study and also by faith.”
  • Directs them to organize a “house of God”
    and led to the School of the Prophets in Kirtland.
  • As the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants was prepared,
    four additional verses (138–141)     were added to the section.

All Lesson Scriptures (D&C 88)

  1. D&C 88:62–64 — “Draw near unto me”
  2. D&C 88:67–76 — Sanctify yourselves; filled with light
  3. D&C 88:77–80 — Teach one another
  4. D&C 88:118–126 — Seek learning by study and by faith
  5. D&C 88:119 — Establish “a house of God”

Key Verses

  • D&C 88:63 — “Draw near unto me and I will draw near unto you; seek me diligently and ye shall find me; ask, and ye shall receive; knock, and it shall be opened unto you.”
  • D&C 88:67 — “And if your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light; and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things.”
  • D&C 88:118 — “And as all have not faith, seek ye diligently and teach one another words of wisdom; yea, seek ye out of the best books words of wisdom; seek learning, even by study and also by faith.”
  • D&C 88:119 — “Organize yourselves; prepare every needful thing; and establish a house, even a house of prayer, a house of fasting, a house of faith, a house of learning, a house of glory, a house of order, a house of God.”

The “House of God” Pattern (v.119)

  • House of Prayer
  • House of Fasting
  • House of Faith
  • House of Learning
  • House of Glory
  • House of Order
  • House of God

Try one simple practice this week

  • House of Prayer: set a daily 2‑minute prayer (family or personal).
  • House of Learning: read one verse together (or solo) before bed.
  • House of Order: post one spiritual goal on the fridge or in phone notes.

Tip: Keep it easy. Small and steady wins.
